Our Story

Sterling and Victoria

  Her mother had always said that sitting at the back of the classroom was what the “bad kids” did. By that definition, on this particular day, Victoria had decided to be one of those “bad kids” by sitting at a table at the very back of the room. Her grades had been on a steady decline since the end of high school anyway. This class was an algorithms tutorial, and she didn't know anybody in this group of 30 cohorts. The teaching assistant started the class by getting everyone to work together with the people sitting at their table on a set of problems, and so Victoria turned and introduced herself to the two other people at her table. One was a girl, and one was a guy. The guy in her group, named Sterling, laughed at all of her jokes and sarcastic remarks, which was rather flattering; none of her jokes had been good. After tutorial, Sterling stopped Victoria and asked her about the music textbooks she was carrying.
  “I'm taking a music course,” he explained.
  Interest piqued, she asked, “What instruments do you play?”
  “Trumpet, piano, violin, French horn,” he replied, and that sealed the deal. She knew then that she desperately wanted to be his friend. Perhaps she had finally met someone who would gush over Chopin with her and bask in the lush harmonies of Mozart's symphonies. It turned out that Sterling was more of a Bach and Beethoven fan, but now that they were talking, it was only a matter of time before they discovered shared interests in video games, horror movies, and hockey.
